variables

You may be familiar with variables from math class.

x + 1 = 5

In programming, a variable is a name that holds information. The information could be numbers, phrases ("strings"), or more complex sets of data ("objects").

Typically, the name of the variable is on the left, following by an equal sign, then followed by the item being assigned to the variable. In the example below, x is assigned to the number 17.

x = 17

x now refers to the 17 and be used to take place of 17 in all sorts of contexts.
